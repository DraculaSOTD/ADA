CTGAN
Recommended

Best for tabular data with mixed types. Handles non-Gaussian distributions and categorical columns.

High fidelity Mode-specific normalization
How it works:

CTGAN uses conditional GANs with mode-specific normalization to handle non-Gaussian and multimodal distributions. It learns the distribution of your data and generates new samples that maintain statistical properties.

Best for:
  • Mixed data types (numeric, categorical, dates)
  • Financial transaction data
  • Customer demographic data
  • Healthcare records (anonymized)

TimeGAN

Specialized for time series data. Preserves temporal dynamics and long-term dependencies.

Temporal embeddings Sequence preservation
How it works:

TimeGAN combines unsupervised GAN training with supervised learning to capture temporal dynamics. It preserves the stepwise conditional distributions and temporal correlations in time series data.

Best for:
  • Stock market data
  • Sensor readings over time
  • Website traffic patterns
  • Energy consumption data
Advanced Settings:
VAE

Variational Autoencoder for mixed data types. Faster training with good performance.

Fast generation Stable training
How it works:

VAE learns a compressed representation of your data in a latent space, then generates new samples by decoding random points from this space. It provides stable training and fast generation.

Best for:
  • Quick prototyping
  • Smaller datasets
  • Simple tabular data
  • When generation speed is critical
